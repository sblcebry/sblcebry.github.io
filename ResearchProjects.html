Research Projects

I conduct laboratory experiments to investigate the how frictional, material, and stress heterogeneities change earthquake behavior. In particular, I look at heterogeneities due to fluid injection and various fault gouges on large-scale laboratory faults.

Effect of background stress levels on induced seismicity

This study investigated how the initiation and overall size of laboratory earthquakes differed when fluid was pumped into a critically loaded fault (near ready to host an earthquake) verses a faut that was less critical. This is important to understand what natural stress conditions may be favorable to host induced seismicity.

This study was featured by AGU Seismology "Read the paper with the author". Check it out here!

Asperity interactions in quartz gouge

To investigate frictional complexities in the earth, we developed an experimental fault using quartz gouge that develops two dominant seismic asperties with continued shearing. The development of these asperities creates varied and complex behavior.

Seismic swarms from fluid injection into PMMA fault

This work investigates how the initiation of earthquakes differ when they are caused by fluid injection and when they are caused by an application of extenal forces. A particularly interesting observation was that when water was injected onto the fault, it created a swarm of small and large events. This was not observed when the earthquake was caused by external forces.
