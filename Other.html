Volunteering, Hobbies, and Extracirculars

I consider myself a jack-of-all-hobbies, master-of-none which means I like to do a lot of different things with various levels of skill and will happily prattle on about any of them. Please note this list in non-inclusive.

Rowing

Volunteer coach for various programs with age ranges from middle school to adults and skill levels which range from complete beginner to experienced racers. Adult beginners are my favorite. I'm also a volunteer board member at Cascadilla Boat Club where I do a variety of jobs including planning and managing programs, organizing coaches, fixing equipment, and building docks. Within these volunteer roles I seek to make rowing inclusive and accessible for all. I do this by reducing program costs by increasing efficiency of coaching assignments, equipment usage, and creating reasonable and attainable program goals. I also contribute to the planning of free community outreach programs in partnership with local community centers. And when I'm not doing all that, I row (and occasionally race) myself.

Knitting

I love to knit and I think anyone who likes to fiddle with their hands should give knitting a go. Knitting encompasses many engineering principals as you take a 1D piece of string and turn it into a 3D object. In knitting it is important to understand how different stitches, needles, and yarn all affect the physical properties of the finished product (ex., stretch, thickness, warmth, rigidity). My favorite thing to make are hats since they are easy to customize for each indented recipent. One base pattern and an understanding of mechanics can result in endless (ok, actually so far only six) different results.

Sometimes I like to excert significant effort to walk up mountains really slowly. The canine company is always good. Usually the views are too.

Woodworking

I like to design and build useful household items such as a cupboard, bathroom vanity, and bed frame. I put like to put some creativity into how the pieces will fit together and how to best utilize the space for the item and how to make the item useful. Is it cost effective? Probably not. Is it time efficient? Definitely not.

Sewing and Quilting

What I really like about sewing is the ability to customize everything you make. Similar to knitting, sewing encompasses many engineering principals. Projects include a couple quilts, pillows, many bags of various sizes, and simple clothing.
